Uncover the story of the post WW2 Ukrainian-Polish population transfer

context

The year is 1944. After 3 months of combat during a typically exhausting European summer, The Battle of Normandy (June 1944 - August 1944) results in liberation of Western Europe from the control of Nazi Germany.

Some 2500km from Normandy, the victory was enough to bring relief to the forefront of most Ukrainian minds. However, having lost 8 million to WW2 ensured confusion and skepticism echoed through the country.

On September 9th 1944, government of the USSR and the newly formed Polish Committee of National Liberation signed in Lublin the Treaty on evacuation of Ukrainian population from the territory of Poland and Polish citizens from the territory of the USSR.
